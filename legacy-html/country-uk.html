Why UK?

Top Reasons to Study Here

Prestige & History

Oxford and Cambridge are among the oldest and most prestigious universities in the world, and both are QS-ranked globally. A UK degree is recognised and respected everywhere.

Shorter Course Duration

UK undergraduate degrees are 3 years (not 4), and most Master's degrees are just 12 months — saving significant tuition fees and living costs compared to USA or Australia.

Graduate Route Visa

After completing a UK degree, eligible graduates can stay under the Graduate visa: 2 years if applying on or before 31 Dec 2026, 18 months from 1 Jan 2027, or 3 years for PhD/doctoral graduates.

London & Beyond

Study in world cities like London, Edinburgh, Manchester, or Bristol — vibrant, multicultural, safe, and with outstanding career opportunities across all sectors.

Requirements at a Glance

What You Need

  • Valid passport (6+ months)
  • Academic transcripts (SLC, +2, Bachelor's) with translations
  • IELTS 6.5+ (most universities)
  • UCAS personal statement (undergraduate)
  • Statement of Purpose (postgraduate)
  • 2–3 academic/professional references
  • Proof of funds (tuition + living costs: £1,529/month in London or £1,171/month outside London, up to 9 months)
  • CAS (Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies)
  • IHS (Immigration Health Surcharge) payment

Visa Process

How to Get Your Student Visa

Apply via UCAS
For undergraduate, apply through UCAS (up to 5 choices). For postgraduate, apply directly to the university. Oxford International helps with personal statements and references.
Receive Confirmation of Acceptance (CAS)
Your university issues a Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) number once you have an unconditional offer and pay a deposit.
Apply for Student Visa online
Submit your Student Visa application online on the UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI) portal using your CAS number.
Pay Immigration Health Surcharge
Pay the IHS (£776/year) as part of your visa application — this gives you access to the NHS during your studies.
Biometrics appointment
Attend a biometrics appointment at VFS Global, Kathmandu. Bring all original documents.
Receive visa & depart
Visas are typically decided within 3 weeks. Oxford International provides a full pre-departure briefing.
Scholarships

Funding Opportunities

Chevening Scholarship
Fully funded by the UK Government — covers tuition, living stipend, and flights. For outstanding students with leadership potential. Applied through Chevening.org.
Commonwealth Scholarships
Fully funded for students from Commonwealth countries including Nepal — covers all costs. Applied through the Commonwealth Scholarship Commission.
University Scholarships
Russell Group universities offer merit scholarships of GBP 2,000–30,000. Competitive — Oxford International helps identify best-fit universities for scholarship eligibility.
FAQ

Common Questions about Studying in UK

What IELTS score is needed for UK universities?
Most UK universities require IELTS 6.5 overall with no band below 6.0 for undergraduate, and IELTS 6.5–7.0 for postgraduate. Prestigious universities like Oxford and Cambridge require 7.0–7.5.
What is the UCAS points system?
UCAS uses a Tariff Points system to evaluate your qualifications. A-levels, IB, and equivalent qualifications are converted to UCAS points. +2 or A-level results are accepted. Our counsellors help you understand your entry requirements.
Is the Graduate Route visa available to Nepali students?
Yes. Eligible Nepali graduates can apply for the UK Graduate visa. It allows 2 years if applying on or before 31 Dec 2026, 18 months from 1 Jan 2027, or 3 years for PhD/doctoral graduates — no job offer required.
Is London expensive to live in?
London is more expensive than other UK cities. Manchester, Edinburgh, Leeds, and Glasgow offer excellent universities at much lower cost of living — typically £800–1,200/month vs £1,500–2,500 in London.
